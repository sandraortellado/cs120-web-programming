My Travels!

Christmas in Kawaii

December was filled with lots of turtle watching, snorkeling, and a lovely family wedding. I caught a few scrapes on the reef, but it was worth it to catch some great waves and cool fish! Afterwards, I was excited to show my parents Oahu for the first time, and for them to enjoy their first beach vacation in many years.

My Favorite Restaurant!

State Bird Provisions

One of the few affordable michelin star restaurants, State Bird Provisions is a fun experience where you can choose from tons of creative and surprising small dishes to mix and match. A great way to try something new and expand your palate with expertly crafted and curated options. Atmosphere is a bit hectic since it's popular and kind of small with lots of chefs roaming around offering small bites, but I guess that's part of the fun. Conversely, I also love Bouche for its intimate, cozy atmosphere.

My Favorite Food!

Sushi is the perfect balance of fresh, delicate flavors and satisfying textures. It's beautiful, tasty, and fun to eat. I love to eat it alongside edamame, tea, and miso soup.

My Favorite Books!

In no particular order...
Book Title Cover Image Author Summary
Invisible Man Ralph Ellison Via Cliff Notes: Invisible Man is the story of a young, college-educated black man struggling to survive and succeed in a racially divided society that refuses to see him as a human being. Told in the form of a first-person narrative, Invisible Man traces the nameless narrator's physical and psychological journey from blind ignorance to enlightened awareness — or, according to the author, "from Purpose to Passion to Perception" — through a series of flashbacks in the forms of dreams and memories. Set in the U.S. during the pre-Civil Rights era when segregation laws barred black Americans from enjoying the same basic human rights as their white counterparts, the novel opens in the South (Greenwood, South Carolina), although the majority of the action takes place in the North (Harlem, New York)
Jane Eyre Charlotte Brontë Via Goodreads: Charlotte Brontë tells the story of orphaned Jane Eyre, who grows up in the home of her heartless aunt, enduring loneliness and cruelty. This troubled childhood strengthens Jane's natural independence and spirit - which prove necessary when she finds employment as a governess to the young ward of Byronic, brooding Mr Rochester. As her feelings for Rochester develop, Jane gradually uncovers Thornfield Hall's terrible secret, forcing her to make a choice. Should she stay with Rochester and live with the consequences, or follow her convictions - even if it means leaving the man she loves? A novel of intense power and intrigue, Jane Eyre dazzled readers with its passionate depiction of a woman's search for equality and freedom.
The Portrait of Dorian Gray Oscar Wilde Via Goodreads: Enthralled by his own exquisite portrait, Dorian Gray exchanges his soul for eternal youth and beauty. Influenced by his friend Lord Henry Wotton, he is drawn into a corrupt double life, indulging his desires in secret while remaining a gentleman in the eyes of polite society. Only his portrait bears the traces of his decadence.
Things Fall Apart Chinua Achebe Via Goodreads: Enthralled by his own exquisite portrait, Dorian Gray exchanges his soul for eternal youth and beauty. A simple story of a "strong man" whose life is dominated by fear and anger, Things Fall Apart is written with remarkable economy and subtle irony. Uniquely and richly African, at the same time it reveals Achebe's keen awareness of the human qualities common to men of all times and places.
